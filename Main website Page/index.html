Who is Ironman?

Iron Man is a legendary superhero from Marvel Comics, co-created by writer and editor Stan Lee, with scripter Larry Lieber and artists Don Heck and Jack Kirby contributing to his development and design. First appearing in Tales of Suspense, Iron Man later starred in his own comic series. In 1963, Tony Stark's Iron Man joined forces with Thor, Ant-Man, Wasp, and the Hulk to become a founding member of the iconic Avengers team.

Which Cinematic Universe Does He Belong?

Iron Man (real name Anthony Edward "Tony" Stark) is a superhero from Marvel Comics and the Marvel Cinematic Universe, first appearing in the 2008 film Iron Man. He is also one of the protagonists of the TV series The Avengers: Earth's Mightiest Heroes and Avengers Assemble.
The Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) is a groundbreaking American media franchise and shared universe focused on superhero films produced by Marvel Studios. These films feature characters from Marvel Comics, creating an interconnected world. Marvel Studios organizes its releases into "Phases," with the first three phases known as The Infinity Saga and the next three phases as The Multiverse Saga. This approach has made the MCU a global entertainment powerhouse.
